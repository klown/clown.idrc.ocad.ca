Resume

Joseph E. Scheuhammer, Ph.D.
email: clown@alum.mit.edu

I have been a software developer for 24 years. I worked first in multimedia CD-ROM, primarily concerned with problems of coordination: How to organize text, sound, graphics, and animation such that they are presented coherently and in a timely fashion. Since 1996, I have worked in the field of adaptive technology developing alternative user interfaces for persons with disabilities.

I enjoy approaching problems through analysis: Taking the definition of a problem, givens, and the desired outcome as a starting point, explore possible solutions, and determine are more effective. *And bang away at it until a solution is found*.

Employment

Inclusive Design Research Centre, OCAD University, Toronto Ontario, Canada

(Formerly: Adaptive Technology Resource Centre, University of Toronto)

1996 - present

Senior Inclusive Developer.

The IDRC works with industry and standards bodies to influence the design of information technology. The goal is to create solutions that are universally accessible, and, at the same time, commercially feasible. Areas of research include: access to the Internet and the World Wide Web, alternative computer display systems, and alternative computer control methods.

Discis Knowledge Research Incorporated, Toronto Ontario, Canada.

1988 - 1996

Staff programmer.

Discis produced and marketed two kinds of multimedia interactive CD-ROM titles. "Discis Books" were electronic versions of books designed to help children learn how to read. Examples include Beatrix Potter's The Tale of Peter Rabbit and Edgar Allan Poe's The Tell-Tale Heart. The second product line was entertainment oriented; titles include Jewels of the Oracle and Karma: Curse of the 12 Caves. These were games similar to 7thGuest and Myst where players navigate an underground complex and solve puzzles.
